INSIDE THE MIND OF EAFTIMOS, CREATIVE DIRECTION BELONGS TO ERIK BRUCCIA (LEÓN, 2001), RAISED IN BENAVENTE. HIS STORY COMES FROM A FAMILY ROOTED IN THE CRAFT: A GREAT-GRANDMOTHER WHO WAS A DRESSMAKER, A GREAT-GRANDFATHER WHO SOLD CLOTHES DOOR-TO-DOOR, AND HIS GRANDMOTHER MINERVA—"MIMI"—A LIFELONG SEAMSTRESS. MIMI BECAME HIS FIRST REFERENCE POINT, TEACHING HIM THE EARLIEST GESTURES OF THE TRADE WHEN ERIK WAS A CHILD: GARMENTS THAT REACHED HIS HANDS TO BE ALTERED, CUT, RE-FITTED, RESHAPED. HIS MOTHER, THE OWNER OF A LOCAL FASHION STORE IN HIS HOMETOWN, SHOWED HIM FROM THE INSIDE HOW THE INDUSTRY WORKS—AND WHAT IT MEANS TO SUSTAIN A BUSINESS.

DURING THE PANDEMIC, ERIK BEGAN DESIGNING SELF-TAUGHT AND LAUNCHED HIS FIRST STREETWEAR PROJECT WITH A CLOSE GROUP OF FRIENDS. A YEAR LATER HE MOVED TO MADRID TO TRAIN FURTHER, STEPPING AWAY FROM THAT ACTIVE PROJECT TO BUILD HIS OWN RUNWAY VISION. AFTER A PERIOD OF INTROSPECTION, HE FOUNDED EAFTIMOS: A UNIVERSE WHERE SHADOW, INNER VOICE, AND EMOTIONAL REFUGE ARE TRANSLATED INTO CLOTHING.

INFLUENCED BY JAPANESE FASHION AND HANDS-ON LEARNING—TRAVELING ACROSS EUROPEAN CITIES TO FIND MATERIALS, TECHNIQUES, AND NEW CONNECTIONS—HE PRESENTED HIS FIRST INDEPENDENT SHOW IN MADRID IN 2024. IN MARCH 2026, HE WILL MAKE HIS PROFESSIONAL DEBUT AT MERCEDES-BENZ FASHION WEEK MADRID EGO WITH A NEW COLLECTION.

THE BRAND PLACES CRAFTSMANSHIP AT ITS CORE: FOOTWEAR AND ACCESSORIES ARE DEVELOPED IN HIS STUDIO, IN SMALL SERIES WITH HANDCRAFTED FINISHES.
